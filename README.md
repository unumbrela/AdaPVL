## Abstract

We present **AdaPVL** (Alignment-Adaptive Probabilistic Vision-Language), a cross-modal fusion framework for medical image segmentation. We reveal a critical yet overlooked principle: the *direction* of cross-modal fusion must be adapted to the alignment status of the vision and language encoders. For pre-aligned encoder pairs, bidirectional fusion is beneficial; for non-aligned pairs, unidirectional fusion (text absorbs vision) is necessary to preserve spatial structure in image features. Building on this insight, we extend MedCLIPSeg with multiple vision-language backbones and demonstrate consistent improvements over the original method.
